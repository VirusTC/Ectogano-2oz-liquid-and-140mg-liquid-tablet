Clinical Treatment Timeline Standardization

A "six-month" regimen is mathematically standardized using either a strict **180-day calendar cycle** or a **24-week (168-day) clinical cycle**. Both options are calculated below to ensure your hospital compliance templates match standard federal registry data formatting.

* * * * *

1\. Dosage Equations & Pharmacological Mass Formulas

Formulation A: 140mg Oil Tablet

-   **Daily Dose Equation:**\
    \(\text{Daily\ Dose}=2\text{\ tablets/day}\times 140\text{\ mg/tablet}=280\text{\ mg/day}\)
-   **Total Inventory Needed (180-Day Standard):**\
    \(\text{Total\ Units}=2\text{\ tablets/day}\times 180\text{\ days}=360\text{\ tablets}\)\
    \(\text{Total\ Cumulative\ Mass}=360\text{\ tablets}\times 140\text{\ mg}=50,400\text{\ mg\ }(50.4\text{\ g})\)
-   **Total Inventory Needed (24-Week / 168-Day Protocol):**\
    \(\text{Total\ Units}=2\text{\ tablets/day}\times 168\text{\ days}=336\text{\ tablets}\)\
    \(\text{Total\ Cumulative\ Mass}=336\text{\ tablets}\times 140\text{\ mg}=47,040\text{\ mg\ }(47.04\text{\ g})\)

Formulation B: 2 oz Liquid Extract

*Standard US clinical fluid conversions dictate that \(1 \text{ fl oz} \approx 29.5735 \text{ mL}\).*

-   **Daily Dose Equation:**\
    \(\text{Daily\ Volume}=2\text{\ fl\ oz/day}\times 29.5735\text{\ mL/fl\ oz}=59.147\text{\ mL/day}\)
-   **Total Inventory Needed (180-Day Standard):**\
    \(\text{Total\ Volume\ (fl\ oz)}=2\text{\ fl\ oz/day}\times 180\text{\ days}=360\text{\ fl\ oz}\)\
    \(\text{Total\ Volume\ (mL)}=360\text{\ fl\ oz}\times 29.5735\text{\ mL/fl\ oz}=10,646.46\text{\ mL\ }(10.65\text{\ L})\)
-   **Total Inventory Needed (24-Week / 168-Day Protocol):**\
    \(\text{Total\ Volume\ (fl\ oz)}=2\text{\ fl\ oz/day}\times 168\text{\ days}=336\text{\ fl\ oz}\)\
    \(\text{Total\ Volume\ (mL)}=336\text{\ fl\ oz}\times 29.5735\text{\ mL/fl\ oz}=9,936.70\text{\ mL\ }(9.94\text{\ L})\)
