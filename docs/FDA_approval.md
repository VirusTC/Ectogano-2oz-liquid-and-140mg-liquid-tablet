*Origanum vulgare* and its primary bioactive monoterpenoid phenols, carvacrol and thymol, act via broad multi-target pharmaceutical mechanisms. These molecules demonstrate selective cytotoxicity, inducing death in malignant lines while remaining relatively safe for healthy tissues. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

The primary oncological and pathological pathways mapped in biomedical literature include:

1\. Induction of Programmed Cell Death (Apoptosis & Necroptosis)
----------------------------------------------------------------

Oregano extracts trigger both the intrinsic (mitochondrial) and extrinsic apoptotic pathways in cancer cells: [6, 7]

-   Bcl-2 Family Regulation: Upregulates pro-apoptotic proteins (BAX, BAK, Bid, p53) and downregulates anti-apoptotic markers (Bcl-2, Bcl-xL). [8, 9]
-   Mitochondrial Dysregulation: Promotes the loss of mitochondrial membrane potential, triggering the release of Cytochrome C into the cytoplasm. [10, 11]
-   Caspase Cascade: Activates initiator caspase-9 and executioner caspase-3, causing enzymatic cleavage and cell death. [11, 12]
-   Necroptosis Pathways: Activates programmed necrosis (necroptosis) alternative pathways when regular apoptotic mechanisms fail. [13]

2\. Proliferation Arrest and Cell Cycle Inhibition
--------------------------------------------------

Oregano components disrupt the uncontrolled replication of malignant cells: [14]

-   Signal Cascade Suppression: Inhibits critical survival pathways, notably the PI3K/AKT/mTOR, MAPK/ERK, and Wnt/β-catenin signaling cascades.
-   Cell Cycle Checkpoint Arrest: Forces cell cycle accumulation and arrest at the G0/G1 and G2/M phases.
-   Enzymatic Blockade: Acts as a SphK1 inhibitor in lung cancers and exhibits direct anti-EGFR activity. [8, 14, 15, 16]

3\. Inhibition of Angiogenesis, Invasion, and Metastasis
--------------------------------------------------------

Oregano compounds counter the structural progression and tissue remodeling required for tumors to expand: [17]

-   Angiogenic Disruption: Modulates the HIF-1α/VEGF (hypoxia-inducible factor/vascular endothelial growth factor) pathway, starving tumors of new blood vessel growth. [3, 8, 18]
-   Extracellular Matrix Preservation: Downregulates Matrix Metalloproteinases (MMP-1, MMP-2, MMP-9), effectively blocking cancer cell migration, invasion, and metastatic potential. [10, 17]
-   E-cadherin Upregulation: Inhibits epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), ensuring cells maintain adhesion and reducing detachment capabilities. [10, 19]

4\. Pathological Redirection of Redox and Lipogenesis States
------------------------------------------------------------

-   Oxidative Stress Generation: Intentionally drives intracellular reactive oxygen species (ROS) accumulation and glutathione oxidation specifically within cancer cells, causing severe DNA strand breaks (γH2AX activation). [2, 6]
-   Lipogenesis Suppression: Downregulates fatty acid pathway proteins and SREBP1 (sterol regulatory element-binding protein 1), shutting down the lipid biosynthesis necessary to construct cancer cell membranes. [9, 11]

5\. Immunomodulatory and Anti-inflammatory Activity
---------------------------------------------------

-   Biomarker Suppression: Strongly suppresses pro-tumorigenic inflammatory biomarkers, including MCP-1, VCAM-1, ICAM-1, and M-CSF.
-   COX and LOX Pathway Inhibition: Inhibits the 5-LOX, COX-1, and COX-2 enzymes, significantly dampening the inflammatory microenvironment that promotes tumor progression. [17, 20, 21]

Summary of Targeted Cancer Types (Preclinical Models)
-----------------------------------------------------

| Target Cancer Type | Primary Action Mode |
| Breast Cancer (MCF-7, MDA-MB-231) | Inhibits PIK3CA, activates pro-caspases-3/-9, selective cytotoxicity. |
| Gastrointestinal & Colon (Caco2, HT-29) | Drives glutathione oxidation, alters cell redox balance, arrests cell cycles. |
| Melanoma (B16-F10, A375) | Triggers DNA breakage, suppresses melanogenesis, induces necroptosis. |
| Prostate Cancer (PC-3) | Suppresses SREBP1 to prevent lipid-dependent proliferation. |
